## Observations from the verified data (Phase 0 findings)

These are facts read directly from the table above, with modelling
consequences we will carry into later phases:

1. **All 12 candidate sessions load with complete lap, compound, pit-stop,
   track-status and weather data.** The MVP scope (Monaco, Singapore,
   Barcelona, Suzuka × 2023-2025) is therefore frozen.

2. **Lap accuracy is high but not total** — the `IsAccurate` flag covers
   ~80-94% of laps depending on the race (worst: Japan 2023 at 702/880 ≈ 80%).
   Phase 1 will quantify exclusions per category (in/out laps, SC laps,
   inaccurate laps) in the data quality report.

3. **The "high-SC circuit" reputation is weaker in this 3-season window than
   folklore suggests.** Monaco shows a single full SC in three years (2024,
   plus its lap-1 red flag); Singapore's famous 100% SC streak is broken in
   this window (no SC in 2024 nor 2025). Consequence: with ~3 races per
   circuit, frequentist SC rates are nearly uninformative — the Phase 3 model
   must treat uncertainty explicitly (e.g. Beta-Binomial with credible
   intervals), and we should consider extending the SC-history window to
   older seasons (SC causes are mostly circuit-geometry driven, less
   regulation-dependent than tyre behaviour). Decision deferred to Phase 3
   scoping with Mohammed.

4. **Monaco 2023 was a mixed-conditions race** (INTERMEDIATE and WET
   compounds present). Wet-weather laps will be segregated in Phase 1;
   the MVP degradation model targets dry compounds, with wet handling
   documented as out of scope unless the data proves sufficient.

5. **Two lap-1 red flags in the set** (Monaco 2024, Japan 2024). Red flags
   allow free tyre changes, which breaks naive stint arithmetic — the
   ingestion layer must mark red-flag-affected stints explicitly.

6. **TrackStatus granularity varies wildly** (1 row for Spain 2023 — a race
   that never left green-flag running — vs 29 rows for Monaco 2023). A single
   row means "status never changed", not "data missing"; the parser must not
   confuse the two.
